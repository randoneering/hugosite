Fractional IT Infrastructure Support

I help startups and small businesses build, improve, and stabilize the infrastructure their business depends on.

Some teams need to build from scratch. Some need to clean up an environment that grew fast and never got enough structure. Some need a senior engineer to step in for a season and help them get through a migration, reliability issue, or operational mess without making a full-time hire.

That is the work I do through Randoneering, LLC.

I focus on practical infrastructure work: cloud environments, automation, systems design, operational cleanup, and the documentation teams need to maintain what gets built. I come in, figure out what matters, do the work, and leave your team in a better position than I found it.

Who This Is For

I work best with:

  • startups that need infrastructure built without hiring a full-time senior engineer
  • small businesses dealing with aging, inconsistent, or fragile systems
  • teams that need more structure and fewer one-off fixes
  • companies stuck behind a migration, cleanup project, or reliability problem
  • leaders who know something needs attention but do not want to overbuild

Ways I Can Help

Build From The Ground Up

If you are starting fresh, I can design and build the infrastructure your business needs.

That can include:

  • cloud and multi-cloud environment setup
  • baseline infrastructure design
  • Infrastructure as Code
  • automation and repeatable provisioning
  • operational standards and system structure
  • documentation for long-term maintenance

The goal is simple: build something your business can run and grow with.

Improve What You Already Have

A lot of environments work just well enough until they do not.

Maybe the systems grew fast. Maybe nobody documented anything. Maybe every change feels riskier than it should. I help clean that up.

That can include:

  • infrastructure cleanup and modernization
  • reducing drift and inconsistency
  • standardizing environments and workflows
  • improving reliability and recovery
  • tightening documentation and handoff
  • making systems easier to operate

Provide Fractional Support

Some teams do not need a large project. They need experienced help on a part-time basis.

I provide fractional support for companies that need senior infrastructure help without committing to a full-time hire. That might mean advising on architecture, handling operational work, supporting internal teams, or helping you get through a period of change.

Some clients need a few focused weeks. Others need a steady hand for a few months.

How I Work

I keep the process simple. I assess the situation, focus on what matters, and get the work done.

I do not like unnecessary complexity. I do not like building systems that only make sense to the person who built them. And I do not like leaving clients with undocumented work and a vague handoff.

What sets my work apart is straightforward: I do the job, document it clearly, and make sure your team has the context it needs to maintain what I built. If you want ongoing support, I can provide it. But my goal is not to create dependency. My goal is to build capability.

What You Get

When I finish an engagement, you should have:

  • infrastructure that is easier to run
  • decisions that are documented
  • clearer operational processes
  • fewer points of failure
  • enough context to maintain the system with confidence

Why Randoneering

I built Randoneering because a lot of smaller companies sit in the same gap. They have real infrastructure needs, but they are not ready for a full platform team and do not need a bloated consulting engagement.

They need someone who can step in, understand the problem, make smart decisions, and leave behind systems the team can actually support.

That is the gap I like filling.

Let’s Talk

If you need help building infrastructure, cleaning up an existing environment, or getting senior-level support for a project that has stalled out, I would be glad to talk.

The best place to start is a simple conversation about what is going on, what is getting in the way, and what better should look like for your team.
